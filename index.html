HEPI
Research on Cerebral Palsy Hospitalizations at Kenyatta National Hospital

Background:
Cerebral palsy (CP) is a debilitating disease often accompanied by multiple comorbidities, leading to frequent and costly hospitalizations.

Objectives:
Our study aimed to characterize acute causes of hospitalization, costs, length of stay, and associated chronic comorbidities among children aged 2-13 years living with cerebral palsy admitted at Kenyatta National Hospital (KNH).

Methods:
We conducted a retrospective cross-sectional study, analyzing 164 medical files of children with CP admitted at KNH from January 2017 to December 2021. Data on sociodemographic characteristics, acute admission diagnoses, chronic comorbidities, admission costs, payment methods, and length of stay were extracted. Python programming language was utilized for data analysis.

Results:
The majority of children were urban dwellers (82%) with working primary caregivers (80%). Common acute admission diagnoses included convulsions (50%) and pneumonia (34%). Chronic comorbidities included respiratory issues (32%), feeding difficulties (32%), seizure disorders (28%), metabolic disorders (23%), and malnutrition (20%). The average in-hospital stay was 16.7 days, with a median cost of Ksh 46,161. Most costs were covered by NHIF (71%), with some families paying out-of-pocket, especially during initial admissions.

Conclusion:
Seizure and respiratory disorders significantly contribute to both acute and chronic morbidity among children with CP, with feeding difficulties and malnutrition exacerbating their conditions. Prolonged hospital stays also escalate costs. Our findings emphasize the importance of assessing children with CP for seizure control, feeding issues, and nutritional status. Additionally, caregivers should receive education on NHIF insurance during outpatient care.
